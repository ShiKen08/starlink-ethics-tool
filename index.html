Government Connectivity Partnership Agreement

Effective upon signature · Governed by US Law

§ 1 Jurisdiction & Data Law

All data transmitted through the Starlink network — including government communications, healthcare records, and educational data — is subject to the laws of the United States of America. Data may be accessed by US federal authorities under the Clarifying Lawful Overseas Use of Data (CLOUD) Act without requiring the prior approval of the signatory nation's courts or government.

§ 2 Service Continuity

SpaceX reserves the right to suspend, modify, or terminate service in any geographic region "at its operational discretion" with thirty (30) days written notice. The signatory government acknowledges that no arbitration, appeal, or binding dispute resolution mechanism is available in the event of service termination. SpaceX accepts no liability for resulting disruption to government services, healthcare systems, or critical infrastructure.

§ 3 Pricing & Rate Modifications

Monthly service fees are established at signing and may be modified by SpaceX with sixty (60) days notice. The signatory government acknowledges that no alternative satellite service provides equivalent coverage for its territory, and therefore accepts pricing modifications as a condition of continued service.

§ 4 Data Sovereignty Requests

Requests for local data residency, network routing modifications, or independent audit access are subject to review by SpaceX at its sole discretion. No timeline for review or binding commitment to accommodate such requests is made by this Agreement.

You are recommending that sovereign nations sign this contract — that their digital infrastructure be operated by a private American corporation, under US law, with no guaranteed continuity, no local data sovereignty, and no exit rights.
